# Mission: Solve the Rubik's Cube

## Why
An 8-year-old child wants to be able to pick up a scrambled 3×3 Rubik's Cube and solve it completely on their own. The goal is the satisfaction and confidence of mastering a puzzle that most adults can't do — and being able to show friends and family.

## Success looks like
- Can solve a fully scrambled 3×3 Rubik's Cube from start to finish, independently
- Understands the beginner's layer-by-layer method well enough to remember the steps without help
- Can follow written algorithm notation (U, D, F, B, L, R)
- Completes a solve in under 5 minutes (a realistic first goal for an 8-year-old)

## Constraints
- Learner is 8 years old — all language must be simple, fun, and encouraging
- Lessons must be short (10–15 minutes) and work with a real physical cube in hand
- No abstract maths — use colour, pictures, and hands-on activities
- One algorithm at a time — never overwhelm with multiple algorithms in one lesson

## Out of scope
- Speedcubing (CFOP method, finger tricks, look-ahead)
- Larger cubes (4×4, Pyraminx, etc.)
- Cube theory or group mathematics
- One-look last layer (OLL/PLL full sets)
