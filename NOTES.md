# Teaching Notes

## Learner profile
- Age: 8 years old
- No prior Rubik's Cube knowledge
- Motivated by the goal of being able to solve it independently

## Teaching preferences
- Short sentences and simple vocabulary throughout
- Encouraging, never discouraging tone — this puzzle is genuinely hard for this age group
- Always have the physical cube in hand during lessons
- Introduce colour names first, notation (U, R, F, etc.) second
- One new algorithm per lesson maximum — never stack algorithms
- Lots of "try it!" hands-on prompts in every lesson

## Lesson sequence (planned)
1. Meet Your Cube (piece types: centers, edges, corners) ← DONE
2. The Face Names (notation: U, D, F, B, L, R + clockwise/anticlockwise)
3. The White Cross (first layer, step 1)
4. White Corners (first layer complete, step 2)
5. Middle Layer Edges (second layer, step 3)
6. The Yellow Cross (last layer start, step 4)
7. The Final Solve (last layer corners + edges, steps 5–7)
