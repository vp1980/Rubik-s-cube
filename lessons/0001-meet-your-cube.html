🧩 Meet Your Cube!

Before you can solve it, you need to understand it.

👋 Hello, future Cube Master!

You have a Rubik's Cube! 🎉 The cube looks really, really hard. But there is a secret: once you understand how the cube works, you can learn to solve it step by step.

In this first lesson, we will look closely at the cube and discover the 3 kinds of pieces inside it. This is the very first thing every solver needs to know.

💡 Tip: Pick up your real Rubik's Cube and hold it while you read! Look for each piece as we describe it.

🎯 3 Kinds of Pieces

Look at one face (one side) of your cube. You will see 9 coloured squares. But those 9 squares belong to 3 completely different types of pieces!

⭐
Centers
6 1 colour each

One sits in the middle of each face.

🟣
Edges
12 2 colours each

They sit between two centers.

🔴
Corners
8 3 colours each

They sit at every corner.

Total pieces: 6 + 12 + 8 = 26 pieces (plus an invisible core inside that holds it together).

⭐ Centers: The Leaders

There are 6 center pieces — one in the middle of each face. Each center has only one colour.

⭐ The center has the gold glow.

Here is the most important fact about center pieces:

🚫 Centers NEVER move!

No matter how many times you spin the faces, the centers always stay in the same spots relative to each other.

Because centers never move, they tell you what colour each face will be when the cube is solved. The face with the white center will be the white face. The face with the red center will be the red face. And so on!

💡 Try it! Spin your cube many times. Can you move the white center to a different spot? You can't! It always stays in the same place.

🟣 Edges: The Go-Betweens

Edge pieces live between two center pieces. Each edge piece shows 2 different colours — one colour for each face it touches.

🟣 The 4 edge squares on one face.

There are 12 edge pieces on the whole cube. Each one connects two faces. For example, the edge piece between the white face and the red face has a white side AND a red side.

To solve the cube, every edge piece has to go to its correct home — between the two centers that match its colours.

💡 Try it! Find an edge piece on your cube. Tip it to see both colours. Now find the two center pieces with those same colours — that is where this edge needs to go!

🔴 Corners: The Three-Colour Pieces

Corner pieces live at the eight corners of the cube. Each corner piece shows 3 different colours — one for each face it touches.

🔴 The 4 corner squares on one face.

There are 8 corner pieces on the whole cube. Each corner connects three faces at once!

Just like edges, every corner has a correct home — the corner where all three of its colours match the three center pieces nearby.

💡 Try it! Find a corner piece on your cube. Can you see all 3 colours? Now find the three center pieces with those same colours — that is where this corner belongs!

📋 Putting It All Together

Here is one face of a solved cube. Can you spot all three piece types?

⭐ Gold = center   🟣 Purple = edges   🔴 Pink = corners

On each face you can see: 1 center, 4 edges, and 4 corners. That is 9 squares — the whole face!

🌟 The Solving Secret

Now you know the Golden Rule: centers never move. So what does solving the cube really mean?

🏠 Solving the cube = sending every piece home.

Every edge and corner has a correct home — the spot where its colours match the nearby centers. When ALL pieces are home, the cube is solved!

Think of it like a jigsaw puzzle. You are just putting every piece into its correct spot — without knocking the other pieces out of place. That is the whole game!

In the next lesson, you will learn the names of the six faces so you can follow the step-by-step instructions for solving.
